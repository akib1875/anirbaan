Tidal Force

Tidal locking, as seen in celestial bodies like the Moon, presents several advantages for harnessing tidal forces to generate power. With one side of the body perpetually facing its parent body, tidal forces become highly predictable and continuous, providing a reliable energy source. This arrangement can amplify the tidal range, increasing the potential energy available for power generation. Tidal locking simplifies infrastructure design, allowing fixed placement of tidal turbines, and can lead to greater energy conversion efficiency due to consistently strong tidal forces. However, the feasibility of tidal power projects also depends on local geographical factors and environmental considerations.

In a distant galaxy, we witnessed a black hole extracting energy, defying conventional understanding. This phenomenon, detected using the X-ray Multi-Mirror Mission satellite, involves a supermassive black hole in our galaxy. Energy is transferred from the black hole to its surroundings, challenging the idea that black holes only consume energy. This discovery also explain particle jets in quasars, expanding our understanding of these cosmic enigmas. The intense gravity around black holes twists space, causing magnetic fields to tighten and heat the area to extreme temperatures. These findings illuminate the dynamic nature of black holes and their profound impact on the universe.
